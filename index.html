About

Ehab Barbary

Tour Guide and Manager of Egypt Odyssey

Greetings, dear friends! My name is Ehab, and for the past 18 years, I have been working as a tour guide fluent in both English and French. I was born and raised in Luxor, a city located in Upper Egypt that boasts incredible monuments and artifacts from the Pharaonic civilization, such as the temples of Karnak and Luxor and the tombs of legendary pharaohs. It brings me great joy to connect the present with the past, and to showcase the magnificence and splendor of Egypt to visitors from all corners of the world. Whether it's exploring Cairo, Alexandria, Luxor, or Aswan, or soaking up the sun on the stunning beaches of the Red Sea in Hurghada, Sharm El-Sheikh, or Marsa Alam, there is something truly special about experiencing Egypt's wonders firsthand.

Tut Ankh Amon

Tutankh Amun, the young pharaoh of ancient Egypt, ruled with curiosity and ambition. One day, while exploring a hidden chamber within his grand palace, he discovered a mysterious golden amulet. Intrigued by its beauty, he placed it around his neck.

Itinerary

day Itinerary name
Tutankh amon
Day 1 Arrive to CAI: Welcome to Egypt! You’ll meet your Egypt Odyssey travel representative at Cairo airport, with the rest of the day free to explore the city. Welcome Drink.
Day 2 Giza pyramid complex, Saqqara & Memphis: explore the magnificent Giza Pyramid Complex (start your tour by the great pyramid of cheops(2551-2528 BC),is built of an estimated 2.5 million blocks, rising to 137m.then move to Panorama area, enjoy fabulous photos for the three Pyramids, Next move to the sphinx) the captivating Saqqara (the world’s first building in stone, Saqqara, there can be few archaeological sites in all the world, let alone Egypt, to compare with Saqqara)- Lunch at local restaurant in Cairo, continue your Cairo tour by visiting Memphis,( Egyptian Mennefer, was the royal residence and capital of Egypt during the early dynastic period and Old Kingdom and a city of major importance throughout the rest of its history,). and the legendary city of Memphis,( the ruins of the ancient capital of Memphis today lie around the modern village of Mit Rahina) Later you will be driven back to your hotel in Cairo. Overnight Cairo.
Day 3 Cairo to Luxor: Transfer to Cairo Airport to fly to Luxor. Getting to the cruise and check-in, after that You’ll meet your guide and travel to the Temple of Luxor on the city’s eastern bank, dedicated to the worship of the god Amun. You’ll also visit the Karnak temples before you board the cruise boat that will be your lodging for the next few nights. Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner.
Day 4 Luxor to Edfu: After breakfast on board, transfer to visit the classic attractions at the Valley of the Kings, including the Temple of Hatshepsut and Memnon statues. Get back on board and sail to Edfu as you enjoy lunch and afternoon tea on the sundeck, including a trip through the Esna Lock. End the night with dinner and a chance to try traditional Egyptian clothing onboard as you dock overnight in Edfu. Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner.
Day 5 Edfu to Aswan: In the morning you’ll go ashore after breakfast to travel to the Temple of Horus. The temple to the falcon god was once covered by silt during the flooding of the Nile, but it has since been restored and sits as one of the most beautiful and best-preserved temples of the Ptolemaic era. by horse cart. Sail to Kom Ombo next, where you’ll disembark once more to visit the double temples of Sobek and Haroeris. Then get back on board to sail to Aswan, where you’ll anchor for the night. Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner.
Day 6 Aswan and Philae Temple Arrive in Aswan and visit the High Dam, an engineering marvel that helped control the flow of the Nile River. Explore the beautiful Philae Temple, dedicated to the goddess Isis, which was relocated to its current location on Agilkia Island. Enjoy a relaxing boat ride on a traditional felucca around Elephantine Island and the botanical gardens of Kitchener Island
Day 7 Optional Abu Simbel – Head back to Cairo: Early morning, transfer to Abu Simbel (3 hrs each way by road, pre-bookable $110 Per Person), home to impressive temples including those of Ramses II and his wife Nefertari, a temple she shares with the goddess Hathor. The Temple of Ramses II is particularly interesting, home to four statues of its namesake ruler that are illuminated by the sun coming through the temples twice a year, on the days of his birth and appointment as ruler. After the temples, return to Aswan and head back to Cairo. enjoy your final night in Cairo at your leisure. Meals: Breakfast, Lunch.
Day 8 Day: Depart Cairo Enjoy one more Egyptian breakfast before your transfer to the airport. Meals: Breakfast

Offer 1:

East Bank


Luxor temple

Karnak Temple

On the eastern bank of the Nile River in the city of Luxor is located one of the most beautiful and ancient monuments in the world, which is the Karnak Temple or Karnak Temples. It is considered a collection of temples in the Pharaonic civilization of Egypt. It is the main seat of the worship of the god Amun Ra, the very important sun god in the city of Thebes (the ancient name of Luxor now). In this ancient city, we will see how the ancient Egyptian built his civilization that made him immortal over time. This visit will not be complete except by visiting the Luxor Temple, 3 kilometers south of the Karnak temples. There we will see the celebration hall and how the ancient Egyptian lived in a state of joy and celebration of the arrival of this great god. We will feel the value of this civilization during your stay in Luxor .

Offer 2:

West Bank


Valley of the Kings

Temple of Hatshepsut

Collosi of Memnon

In the west of Luxor, there we will visit the eternal homes of the kings of Egypt, which are known as the tombs of the great pharaohs. In fact, they are not tombs as much as they are underground royal palaces, as there are 62 tombs for the rulers of ancient Egypt, such as Ramesses II, Thutmose III, Tutankhamun, and others. There we will see the true greatness of this civilization and what it is. For them, the subject of death is merely a journey that will take them to a better world, After that, we will visit the temple of Queen Hatshepsut, who ruled Egypt for 27 years in 1400 BC. This lady was worthy of ruling Egypt, as she showed a lot of greatness and glory throughout that period in the life of Egypt. This temple is built on 3 floors and is considered different from the rest of the temples in the Temple of That queen, we will see bright colors that are pleasing to the eye, and we will learn about how the ancient Egyptian used colors. The third visit on this day is to visit the two statues of Memnon, which are located on the side of the road. Each statue weighs 700 tons. There we will see the history of this place, as behind the two statues there was a very huge temple for King Amenhotep III

Offer 3:

Edfou & Kom Ombo


Edfu

Kom Ombo

On the way from Luxor to Aswan in southern Egypt, you can discover two very beautiful temples in the south of Luxor. You are going to the city of Aswan, which is considered the last governorate in southern Egypt. These two temples are the Temple of Edfu, which is located 120 kilometers south of Luxor and is dedicated to the worship of the god Horus, “the falcon.” There You will know what is the history of this good god who was able to conquer evil represented by his uncle, the evil god Set. This is considered one of the best preserved temples in Egypt. As for the other temple, it is Kom Ombo, which is located 45 kilometers before the city of Aswan, and it is dedicated to the worship of the god Sobek, “the crocodile.” There we will see a museum for preserving crocodile mummies, since this area was the headquarters for worshiping the crocodile, and we will see what was the idea that made the ancient Egyptians worship these animals and make them Gods worthy of respect

Offer 4:

Aswan


High Dam, Aswan

Philae Temple

Unfinished Obelisk

If you are a tourist already residing in the city of Aswan and want to discover the archaeological monuments there, we will help you in achieving your desire. In the city of Aswan, which is about 800 kilometers away from Cairo, and this large city is famous for being the land of gold. In Aswan, we will visit one of the most important ancient Egyptian temples for one of the most important gods is the goddess Isis, the goddess of motherhood and magic. We will learn about her and what she did in her life with her faithful husband, Osiris, on a visit that is considered one of the most wonderful places in Egypt. This temple is unique in its location because it is located on an island in the middle of the Nile. The second visit to a project considered one of the largest projects built in Egypt in the twentieth century, which is the High Dam, the building that protected Egypt from the dangers of flood and is considered a source of electricity generation in Egypt . The third visit is to the granite quarry. There we will see how the ancient Egyptians made the obelisks that they transported to the temples in Luxor and what is the idea behind the obelisk .
